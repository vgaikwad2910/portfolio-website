USA vs Bosnia: A Match Prediction Built on Poisson, Bayes, and FC 26

Vaishnavi Gaikwad · July 2026 · 6 min read · GitHub

My last prediction got stuffed. Turkey-style. 🦃

I ran a Bayesian Poisson model on USA vs Türkiye, declared USA wins 2–0 with confidence, and watched Türkiye win 3–2. The model didn't just miss the result — it missed it in the most dramatic way possible.

Moving on. Round of 32. USA vs Bosnia. This time I upgraded the model.

What's Different This Time

The previous model was entirely team-level — it knew how many goals each team scored and conceded in the group stage, smoothed those numbers toward the tournament average, and ran 100,000 simulated matches. It knew nothing about the actual players on the pitch.

This version adds a player quality layer on top of that foundation. For each team's predicted starting XI, I pulled FC 26 ratings and computed two composites per player:

  • ATK composite = (Shooting + Dribbling) / 2 — for attackers and midfielders
  • DEF composite = Defending rating — for defenders and the goalkeeper

These are averaged across the starting lineup and normalized against a baseline of 72 (roughly the tournament average player). The result is a quality multiplier applied to each team's expected goals (λ) before the Monte Carlo simulation runs.

The idea: group stage stats tell you what a team did. FC 26 composites tell you something about what they're capable of, based on the individuals selected.

The Model, Step by Step

Step 1 — Raw rates from the group stage

Data sourced from FotMob. USA scored 8 goals in 3 games (2.67/game) and conceded 4 (1.33/game). Bosnia scored 5 (1.67/game) and conceded 6 (2.00/game). Tournament average: 1.46 goals per team per game across all group stage matches.

Step 2 — Bayesian smoothing

Three games is a tiny sample. A team that scores 5 goals in one game looks elite; a team that went quiet looks poor. Bayesian smoothing blends each team's rate toward the tournament average using a prior of 3 imaginary "average" games:

smoothed_rate = (raw_rate × n_games + avg × prior) / (n_games + prior)

With prior = 3 and tournament avg = 1.46, a team's 3 real games carry equal weight to 3 imaginary average games. More games → less pulled toward the mean. Fewer games → more conservative estimate.

Step 3 — Dixon-Coles attack/defense strengths

Each team gets an attack strength (how much they score relative to the tournament average) and a defense strength (how little they concede). The expected goals for USA against Bosnia is:

λ_USA = (USA attack strength / avg) × (Bosnia defense strength / avg) × avg

Step 4 — Player quality multiplier

With predicted starting XIs for both teams, FC 26 composites are normalized to a baseline of 72. USA's quality multiplier came out at 1.099 (roughly a 10% boost) and Bosnia's at 1.016. These scale each team's λ up or down before simulation.

Step 5 — Monte Carlo simulation

With λ_USA = 2.69 and λ_Bosnia = 1.52, I draw 100,000 pairs of Poisson random numbers — one for each team in each simulated match. Count up the results. That's it.

Think of it like two bags of numbered balls. USA's bag has "2" and "3" as the most common draws. Bosnia's bag has "1" and "2". Close your eyes, pull one from each, record the result. Do that 100,000 times. You get probabilities.

Most likely scoreline: USA 2–1 at 8.2% probability. The radar tells the broader story — USA leads on attack output and possession, while Bosnia is more physical and defensively compact. If Bosnia can frustrate USA in transition and keep it level, the penalty scenario becomes very real.

What the Model Still Can't See

Still missing:

  • Tactical matchups — high press vs. low block, formation battles
  • Current form and fitness of individual players
  • Injuries or suspensions heading into the match
  • Set piece quality (corners and free kicks are disproportionately impactful)
  • The chaos factor — knockout football doesn't care about expected goals

The player quality layer is an improvement, but it's still a static snapshot. FC 26 ratings don't know that Pulisic played 120 minutes three days ago, or that Bosnia's goalkeeper had the tournament of his life in the group stage.

Let's see if the model survives this one. 🤞
